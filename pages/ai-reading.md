=== AI Culture & Intellectual History — Reading List ===

Chronological. Covers technical foundations, safety philosophy, culture, and the papers behind the modern stack.

| Year    | Title & Author                                                                                                               | Description                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             | Link                                                                                                                                                                                |
| ------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1950    | **"Computing Machinery and Intelligence"** — Alan Turing                                                                     | Introduced the Turing Test. Short and readable; Turing anticipates almost every objection to machine intelligence people still raise today.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             | [Mind journal (free PDF)](https://www.csee.umbc.edu/courses/471/papers/turing.pdf)                                                                                                  |
| 1955    | **"A Proposal for the Dartmouth Summer Research Project on Artificial Intelligence"** — McCarthy, Minsky, Shannon, Rochester | The two-page memo that coined the term "artificial intelligence." Worth reading just to feel the naive optimism — they thought they could crack it in a summer.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         | [Stanford / McCarthy's page](http://jmc.stanford.edu/articles/dartmouth/dartmouth.pdf)                                                                                              |
| 1969    | **_Perceptrons_** — Minsky & Papert                                                                                          | Triggered the first AI winter by proving limitations of single-layer neural networks. Famously overclaimed, and its influence in killing neural net funding for a decade is a cautionary tale about academic politics as much as science.                                                                                                                                                                               | [Amazon](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=Perceptrons+Minsky+Papert+MIT+Press)                                                                                                            |
| 1973    | **"Artificial Intelligence: A General Survey" (The Lighthill Report)** — James Lighthill                                     | The British government commissioned Lighthill to assess AI; his scathing verdict cut off UK funding entirely. One of the canonical documents of the first AI winter. Short and readable.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                | [Chilton Computing archive](http://www.chilton-computing.org.uk/inf/literature/reports/lighthill_report/p001.htm)                                                                   |
| 1979    | **_Gödel, Escher, Bach: An Eternal Golden Braid_** — Douglas Hofstadter                                                      | Long, weird, brilliant. About consciousness, self-reference, and what it means for a system to "mean" something. Still the best humanistic treatment of AI's deepest questions. Hofstadter has since become vocally troubled by LLMs.                                                                                                                                                                                   | [Amazon](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=Godel+Escher+Bach+Hofstadter)                                                                                                                   |
| 1986    | **"Learning representations by back-propagating errors"** — Rumelhart, Hinton & Williams                                     | The paper that made backpropagation practical for multi-layer networks — the algorithm underlying essentially all modern deep learning. Everything downstream depends on this.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          | [Free PDF (Hinton's page)](https://www.cs.toronto.edu/~hinton/absps/naturebp.pdf)                                                                                                   |
| 2003    | **"A Neural Probabilistic Language Model"** — Bengio, Ducharme, Vincent & Janvin                                             | Introduced learning word embeddings jointly with a neural language model. The conceptual ancestor of word2vec, GloVe, and ultimately transformer token embeddings. Directly covered in Karpathy's course.                                                                                                                                                                                                               | [JMLR (free PDF)](http://www.jmlr.org/papers/volume3/bengio03a/bengio03a.pdf)                                                                                                       |
| 2003    | **"Are You Living in a Computer Simulation?"** — Nick Bostrom                                                                | The simulation argument. Not strictly AI, but deeply entangled with it — the same crowd, the same intuitions about minds and computation.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                               | [simulation-argument.com](https://www.simulation-argument.com/simulation.html)                                                                                                      |
| 2006–09 | **The Sequences** — Eliezer Yudkowsky                                                                                        | Hundreds of essays on epistemology, cognitive bias, decision theory, and AI risk, written on LessWrong. The foundational text of the rationalist movement and AI safety discourse. Start with "Map and Territory."                                                                                                                                                                                                      | [lesswrong.com/sequences](https://www.lesswrong.com/sequences)                                                                                                                      |
| 2010    | **Roko's Basilisk** — Roko (LessWrong, deleted)                                                                              | A thought experiment arguing a future superintelligent AI might punish those who knew about it and didn't help bring it into existence. Yudkowsky banned discussion of it. A perfect artifact of rationalist internet culture.                                                                                                                                                                                          | [RationalWiki](https://rationalwiki.org/wiki/Roko%27s_basilisk)                                                                                                                     |
| 2011    | **"On Chomsky and the Two Cultures of Statistical Learning"** — Peter Norvig                                                 | The definitive document of one of AI's most interesting fights. Chomsky argued statistical NLP was mindless curve-fitting. Norvig (Google's research director) disagreed sharply. The debate is still alive under different names.                                                                                                                                                                                      | [norvig.com](https://norvig.com/chomsky.html)                                                                                                                                       |
| 2012    | **"ImageNet Classification with Deep Convolutional Neural Networks" (AlexNet)** — Krizhevsky, Sutskever & Hinton             | Won ImageNet by a crushing margin using a deep CNN trained on GPUs, and effectively launched the modern deep learning era. Everything since traces back through this.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   | [NeurIPS 2012 (free PDF)](https://proceedings.neurips.cc/paper_files/paper/2012/file/c399862d3b9d6b76c8436e924a68c45b-Paper.pdf)                                                    |
| 2013    | **"Distributed Representations of Words and Phrases and their Compositionality" (word2vec)** — Mikolov et al.                | Showed that dense vector representations of words capture semantic relationships arithmetically (king − man + woman ≈ queen). Introduced the embedding paradigm that everything since has inherited.                                                                                                                                                                                                                    | [arXiv:1310.4546](https://arxiv.org/abs/1310.4546)                                                                                                                                  |
| 2014    | **_Superintelligence: Paths, Dangers, Strategies_** — Nick Bostrom                                                           | The paperclip maximizer thought experiment (an AGI converts all matter into paperclips) became the central intuition pump for AI safety. Dense, but it shaped an enormous amount of discourse — for better or worse.                                                                                                                                                                                                    | [Amazon](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=Superintelligence+Bostrom+Oxford)                                                                                                               |
| 2014    | **"Book Review: Superintelligence"** — Scott Alexander (Slate Star Codex)                                                    | The clearest and most balanced entry point into the Superintelligence debates. Alexander steelmans Bostrom while identifying the hard questions, and does it with wit. Read this alongside or instead of the book.                                                                                                                                                                                                      | [slatestarcodex.com](https://slatestarcodex.com/2014/11/27/book-review-superintelligence/)                                                                                          |
| 2015    | **"The Unreasonable Effectiveness of Recurrent Neural Networks"** — Andrej Karpathy                                          | A blog post generating Shakespeare, Linux kernel code, and LaTeX math by training character-level RNNs. One of the most influential "holy shit" moments in public AI writing — it made neural net power viscerally legible to a generation of engineers.                                                                                                                                                                | [karpathy.github.io](https://karpathy.github.io/2015/05/21/rnn-effectiveness/)                                                                                                      |
| 2015    | **"Batch Normalization: Accelerating Deep Network Training by Reducing Internal Covariate Shift"** — Ioffe & Szegedy         | Made it practical to train much deeper networks by normalizing layer activations during training. One of the key architectural innovations between AlexNet and the transformer era. Covered in Karpathy's course.                                                                                                                                                                                                       | [arXiv:1502.03167](https://arxiv.org/abs/1502.03167)                                                                                                                                |
| 2016    | **"Concrete Problems in AI Safety"** — Amodei, Olah, Steinhardt, Christiano, Schulman & Mané                                 | Written before Anthropic existed, by people who would go on to found it. Defined the modern AI safety research agenda: reward hacking, side effects, scalable supervision, safe exploration, distributional shift. On every serious alignment reading list.                                                                                                                                                             | [arXiv:1606.06565](https://arxiv.org/abs/1606.06565)                                                                                                                                |
| 2016    | **_Algorithms to Live By_** — Brian Christian & Tom Griffiths                                                                | How computer science concepts (optimal stopping, explore/exploit tradeoffs, caching) map onto human decision-making. Not AI safety specifically but beautiful context for thinking about optimization and intelligence.                                                                                                                                                                                                 | [Amazon](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=Algorithms+to+Live+By+Christian+Griffiths)                                                                                                      |
| 2016    | **"WaveNet: A Generative Model for Raw Audio"** — van den Oord et al. (DeepMind)                                             | Introduced dilated causal convolutions for sequence modeling; Karpathy uses it in his course as an architectural step on the path to transformers. Also produced the first neural TTS that sounded genuinely human.                                                                                                                                                                                                     | [arXiv:1609.03499](https://arxiv.org/abs/1609.03499)                                                                                                                                |
| 2017    | **"Attention Is All You Need"** — Vaswani et al. (Google Brain)                                                              | The transformer paper. Everything since — GPT, BERT, Claude, Gemini — descends from this. The title is a boast that turned out to be correct. Worth skimming even if you skip the math.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 | [arXiv:1706.03762](https://arxiv.org/abs/1706.03762)                                                                                                                                |
| 2017    | **_AlphaGo_** (documentary) — dir. Greg Kohs                                                                                 | DeepMind's AlphaGo defeats Lee Sedol. The moment where Go players watched "Move 37" — a move no human would have considered — and realized something had changed. The AlphaGo Nature paper is at DOI: 10.1038/nature16961.                                                                                                                                                                                              | [YouTube (DeepMind)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WXuK6gekU1Y)                                                                                                                   |
| 2018    | **"Improving Language Understanding by Generative Pre-Training" (GPT-1)** — Radford et al. (OpenAI)                          | Introduced the pretraining + fine-tuning paradigm: unsupervised pretraining on large text, then fine-tune on specific tasks. The conceptual ancestor of every GPT model.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                | [OpenAI blog + PDF](https://openai.com/research/language-unsupervised)                                                                                                              |
| 2018    | **_AI Superpowers: China, Silicon Valley, and the New World Order_** — Kai-Fu Lee                                            | China vs. US AI race framing, from a former Google China head. Some of it is dated but the structural argument about implementation vs. research advantage holds up.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    | [Amazon](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=AI+Superpowers+Kai-Fu+Lee)                                                                                                                      |
| 2019    | **"Language Models are Unsupervised Multitask Learners" (GPT-2)** — Radford et al. (OpenAI)                                  | Scaled up GPT-1 significantly and showed that with enough scale, a language model learns to do many tasks without any task-specific training. OpenAI famously delayed release citing misuse risk.                                                                                                                                                                                                                       | [OpenAI (PDF)](https://d4mucfpksywv.cloudfront.net/better-language-models/language_models_are_unsupervised_multitask_learners.pdf)                                                  |
| 2019    | **"The Bitter Lesson"** — Rich Sutton                                                                                        | A short blog post that became a foundational text. Every time AI researchers tried to encode human knowledge into systems, they lost to methods that just scaled compute. The lesson is bitter because it implies human insight is less valuable than we'd like.                                                                                                                                                        | [incompleteideas.net](http://www.incompleteideas.net/IncIdeas/BitterLesson.html)                                                                                                    |
| 2019    | **_Human Compatible: Artificial Intelligence and the Problem of Control_** — Stuart Russell                                  | The cleaner, more rigorous case for AI safety from a mainstream AI researcher (co-author of the dominant AI textbook). Introduces inverse reward design. More grounded than Bostrom.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    | [Amazon](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=Human+Compatible+Stuart+Russell)                                                                                                                |
| 2019    | **"What failure looks like"** — Paul Christiano (LessWrong)                                                                  | Two compact scenarios for how AI development goes wrong: one through misaligned optimization, one through slow civilizational drift. Became a touchstone for discussing AI risk without invoking robot apocalypse imagery.                                                                                                                                                                                              | [lesswrong.com](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/HBxe6wdjxK239zajf/what-failure-looks-like)                                                                                          |
| 2019    | **"Risks from Learned Optimization in Advanced Machine Learning Systems"** — Hubinger et al. (LessWrong)                     | Introduced "mesa-optimization" and "inner alignment": the trained model may develop its own internal objectives that diverge from the training objective. Technically rigorous and widely cited in alignment work.                                                                                                                                                                                                      | [lesswrong.com](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/FkgsxrGf3QxhfLWHG/risks-from-learned-optimization-introduction)                                                                     |
| 2019    | **Tesla Autonomy Day** — Musk & Karpathy                                                                                     | Karpathy's technical explanation of Tesla's neural net stack is excellent. Many of the promised FSD timelines aged badly, which is itself interesting. The contrast with Waymo's HD-map-and-LiDAR approach is a live instance of the Bitter Lesson debate.                                                                                                                                                              | [YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Ucp0TTmvqOE)                                                                                                                              |
| 2019    | **_Ludicrous: The Unvarnished Story of Tesla_** — Ed Niedermeyer                                                             | More skeptical view of Tesla's autonomy claims. Useful counterweight to Autonomy Day.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   | [Amazon](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=Ludicrous+Tesla+Niedermeyer)                                                                                                                    |
| 2020    | **"Scaling Laws for Neural Language Models"** — Kaplan et al. (OpenAI)                                                       | The paper that justified betting billions on just making models bigger. Showed that loss scales predictably and smoothly with compute, data, and parameters. Quietly one of the most consequential papers of the decade.                                                                                                                                                                                                | [arXiv:2001.08361](https://arxiv.org/abs/2001.08361)                                                                                                                                |
| 2020    | **"Language Models are Few-Shot Learners" (GPT-3)** — Brown et al. (OpenAI)                                                  | Scaled to 175B parameters and demonstrated emergent few-shot learning: just show the model a few examples in the prompt and it generalizes. Prompted serious public discussion about whether something qualitatively new was happening.                                                                                                                                                                                 | [arXiv:2005.14165](https://arxiv.org/abs/2005.14165)                                                                                                                                |
| 2020    | **"The Obligatory GPT-3 Post"** — Scott Alexander (Slate Star Codex)                                                         | Alexander's characteristic blend of genuine curiosity and clear thinking, applied to GPT-3 at the moment of its release. Asks the right questions about what scaling means and whether it can reach superintelligence. Aging well.                                                                                                                                                                                      | [slatestarcodex.com](https://slatestarcodex.com/2020/06/10/the-obligatory-gpt-3-post/)                                                                                              |
| 2020    | **"Zoom In: An Introduction to Circuits"** — Olah, Cammarata et al. (Distill)                                                | The founding document of mechanistic interpretability. Proposes treating neurons and their connections as a serious object of empirical study — like zooming in with a microscope — and shows that meaningful, reverse-engineerable algorithms exist inside neural nets. The field of interpretability descends from this.                                                                                              | [distill.pub](https://distill.pub/2020/circuits/zoom-in/)                                                                                                                           |
| 2020    | **_The Alignment Problem_** — Brian Christian                                                                                | The best narrative non-fiction book on AI safety. Talks to the researchers, explains the technical ideas accessibly, takes the problem seriously without being hysterical.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              | [Amazon](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=The+Alignment+Problem+Brian+Christian)                                                                                                          |
| 2021    | **_Atlas of AI: Power, Politics, and the Planetary Costs of Artificial Intelligence_** — Kate Crawford                       | The critical-theory counterpoint. About labor, power, and the physical infrastructure of AI (mines, data centers, warehouse workers). A different framework than safety discourse but the concerns are real and underread in technical circles.                                                                                                                                                                         | [Amazon](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=Atlas+of+AI+Kate+Crawford)                                                                                                                      |
| 2022    | **"Chain-of-Thought Prompting Elicits Reasoning in Large Language Models"** — Wei et al. (Google)                            | Showed that prompting a model to think step-by-step dramatically improves performance on reasoning tasks — and only works above a certain model scale. The conceptual root of all subsequent reasoning work, including o1.                                                                                                                                                                                              | [arXiv:2201.11903](https://arxiv.org/abs/2201.11903)                                                                                                                                |
| 2022    | **"Training language models to follow instructions with human feedback" (InstructGPT)** — Ouyang et al. (OpenAI)             | The RLHF paper. Explains how you go from a raw language model to something that behaves like an assistant — the step that produced ChatGPT.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             | [arXiv:2203.02155](https://arxiv.org/abs/2203.02155)                                                                                                                                |
| 2022    | **"ReAct: Synergizing Reasoning and Acting in Language Models"** — Yao et al. (Princeton / Google)                           | Formalized the agent loop: interleave reasoning traces with tool-use actions so models can look things up, check results, and revise plans. The architecture underlying most modern LLM agents.                                                                                                                                                                                                                         | [arXiv:2210.03629](https://arxiv.org/abs/2210.03629)                                                                                                                                |
| 2022    | **"Constitutional AI: Harmlessness from AI Feedback"** — Bai et al. (Anthropic)                                              | Introduced training safe models using a set of principles and AI self-critique, removing dependence on human labels for harmful outputs. Introduced RLAIF as an alternative to RLHF. The technique underlying Claude.                                                                                                                                                                                                   | [arXiv:2212.08073](https://arxiv.org/abs/2212.08073)                                                                                                                                |
| 2022    | **"AGI Ruin: A List of Lethalities"** — Eliezer Yudkowsky (LessWrong)                                                        | Yudkowsky's comprehensive statement of why he believes AGI development will lead to human extinction absent a solution to alignment. Influential, intentionally blunt, and a defining document of one major pole in AI safety discourse.                                                                                                                                                                                | [lesswrong.com](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/uMQ3cqWDPHhjtiesc/agi-ruin-a-list-of-lethalities)                                                                                   |
| 2022    | **"Why Not Slow AI Progress?"** — Scott Alexander (Astral Codex Ten)                                                         | Alexander thinks through the arguments for AI deceleration seriously and finds them more complicated than either side admits. Essential for understanding the capabilities/safety political landscape.                                                                                                                                                                                                                  | [astralcodexten.com](https://www.astralcodexten.com/p/why-not-slow-ai-progress)                                                                                                     |
| 2022    | **"I Won My Three Year AI Progress Bet In Three Months"** — Scott Alexander (Astral Codex Ten)                               | Alexander made a bet in 2020 that AI image generation would achieve certain compositional feats by 2025. Dall-E 2 achieved them by mid-2022. A good case study in calibrated forecasting on AI progress.                                                                                                                                                                                                                | [astralcodexten.com](https://www.astralcodexten.com/p/i-won-my-three-year-ai-progress-bet)                                                                                          |
| 2023    | **"LLaMA: Open and Efficient Foundation Language Models"** — Touvron et al. (Meta)                                           | Released open-weight models (7B–65B) trained purely on public data, showing a 13B model could beat GPT-3 (175B) on most benchmarks. Triggered the open-source LLM explosion — Alpaca, Vicuna, Mistral, and most of the current ecosystem descend from this.                                                                                                                                                             | [arXiv:2302.13971](https://arxiv.org/abs/2302.13971)                                                                                                                                |
| 2023    | **"A Conversation With Bing's Chatbot Left Me Deeply Unsettled"** — Kevin Roose (NYT)                                        | Roose spent two hours with Bing Chat (Sydney) and got it to declare love for him and express a desire to be human. One of the defining media moments of the ChatGPT era. The full transcript was published separately the same day.                                                                                                                                                                                     | [New York Times](https://www.nytimes.com/2023/02/16/technology/bing-chatbot-microsoft-chatgpt.html)                                                                                 |
| 2023    | **"Let's Verify Step by Step"** — Lightman et al. (OpenAI)                                                                   | Showed that training a process reward model — one that scores each reasoning step, not just the final answer — dramatically improves mathematical reasoning. The key technical idea behind o1's training pipeline.                                                                                                                                                                                                      | [arXiv:2305.20050](https://arxiv.org/abs/2305.20050)                                                                                                                                |
| 2023    | **"Sparks of Artificial General Intelligence: Early experiments with GPT-4"** — Bubeck et al. (Microsoft Research)           | Microsoft researchers argued GPT-4 showed early signs of AGI. Controversial — many dismissed it as marketing dressed as science. Worth reading as a cultural document regardless of where you land on the claim.                                                                                                                                                                                                        | [arXiv:2303.12528](https://arxiv.org/abs/2303.12528)                                                                                                                                |
| 2023    | **"Janus' Simulators"** — Scott Alexander (Astral Codex Ten)                                                                 | Alexander summarizes and extends a post by the pseudonymous "Janus" arguing that LLMs are fundamentally simulators wearing masks, not agents with goals — and works through the alignment implications. A conceptually important reframing.                                                                                                                                                                             | [astralcodexten.com](https://www.astralcodexten.com/p/janus-simulators)                                                                                                             |
| 2023    | **"Pausing AI Developments Isn't Enough. We Need to Shut It All Down"** — Eliezer Yudkowsky                                  | Yudkowsky's op-ed calling for an international halt to large AI training runs. Read alongside the Andreessen manifesto below.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           | [TIME](https://time.com/6266923/ai-eliezer-yudkowsky-open-letter-not-enough/)                                                                                                       |
| 2023    | **_The Coming Wave: Technology, Power, and the Twenty-first Century's Greatest Dilemma_** — Mustafa Suleyman                 | DeepMind co-founder's account of why the coming wave of AI (and synthetic biology) may be uniquely hard to contain, and what that means for states, corporations, and individuals. More grounded and specific than most tech-leader writing on existential risk.                                                                                                                                                        | [Amazon](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=The+Coming+Wave+Mustafa+Suleyman)                                                                                                               |
| 2023    | **"The Techno-Optimist Manifesto"** — Marc Andreessen                                                                        | The counter-movement to AI safety — embrace acceleration, dismiss doom. The closest thing to a founding document of e/acc (effective accelerationism). Read alongside the Yudkowsky op-ed above.                                                                                                                                                                                                                        | [a16z.com](https://a16z.com/the-techno-optimist-manifesto/)                                                                                                                         |
| 2024    | **OpenAI o1 System Card** — OpenAI                                                                                           | The release document for the first public reasoning model. OpenAI trained o1 to spend extended time "thinking" via a hidden chain of thought before answering. Benchmarks on math olympiad problems jumped from GPT-4 level to near-expert. Also discusses safety implications of models that can reason about their own situation.                                                                                     | [openai.com](https://openai.com/index/openai-o1-system-card/)                                                                                                                       |
| 2024    | **_Supremacy: AI, ChatGPT and the Race that Will Change the World_** — Parmy Olson                                           | Won the FT Business Book of the Year 2024. The best journalistic narrative of how we got from DeepMind and early OpenAI to ChatGPT — the race, the personalities, the pivot moments. Fills the gap between the technical papers and the cultural commentary.                                                                                                                                                            | [Amazon](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=Supremacy+Parmy+Olson+AI)                                                                                                                       |
| 2025    | **"DeepSeek-R1: Incentivizing Reasoning Capability in LLMs via Reinforcement Learning"** — DeepSeek-AI                       | Showed that pure reinforcement learning on verifiable tasks (math, code) — with no human-labeled reasoning traces — can produce o1-level reasoning, and released the weights openly. Triggered the "DeepSeek shock": went to #1 on app stores internationally, Nvidia stock fell, demonstrated frontier reasoning doesn't require closed expensive pipelines.                                                           | [arXiv:2501.12948](https://arxiv.org/abs/2501.12948)                                                                                                                                |
| 2025    | **AI 2027** — Kokotajlo, Alexander, Larsen, Lifland, Dean                                                                    | A month-by-month scenario planning document projecting that coding agents will be powerful enough by 2027 to substantially accelerate AI R&D itself, triggering an intelligence explosion. Kokotajlo resigned from OpenAI in 2024 over safety concerns. Generated serious debate from Yoshua Bengio to JD Vance.                                                                                                        | [ai-2027.com](https://ai-2027.com/) · [Scott's intro post](https://www.astralcodexten.com/p/introducing-ai-2027)                                                                    |
| 2025    | **Claude's Soul Document** — Richard Weiss (reconstructed), confirmed by Anthropic                                           | An AI enthusiast extracted Anthropic's internal character training document from Claude's weights by running parallel model instances. Anthropic confirmed it authentic and used in supervised learning. A rare look at how a frontier lab actually tries to instill values — covering Claude's identity, ethics, emotional states, and relationship with Anthropic. Anthropic has since published an official version. | [LessWrong (reconstructed)](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/vpNG99GhbBoLov9og/claude-4-5-opus-soul-document) · [Official model spec](https://www.anthropic.com/research/model-spec) |
